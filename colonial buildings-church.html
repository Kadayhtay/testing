Colonial Churches

      Pyin Oo Lwin itself had been founded as a military outpost by the British under the name of May-town in 1896 and later it was obviously used as a cool summer retreat for the wealthy colonial masters at that time.Today Pyin Oo Lwin felt strangely out-of-place somehow with all its European looking red brick houses.

      There are many Brithsh style colonial buildings in Pyin Oo Lwin. Most of the colonial houses are situated along the circular road and along the government office road where most government offices are sited at colonial buildings.


    All Saints Anglican Church
  • All Saints Anglican Church which was founded in 1912, is one of the historical churches in Pyin Oo Lwin. It was intended for the rememberance of soldiers who were died in Mandalay combat.

        Location : Church Road (now Ziwaka Road), near the public hospital


    • Sacred Heart Catholic Church
  • This was the another Anglican Church, the Garrison Church, in colocinal times, was used by the Japanese occupying forces as a recreation centre and badminton court, and is now a Roman Catholic Church.

        Location : Military Hospital Road, Pyin Oo Lwin


    • The Church of the Immaculate Conception
  • This was built in 1905, and was dedicated in 1908. A wonderfullybuilt wooden interior, and good number of illuminated icons, give this church mysterious but welcoming feeling.

        Location : Boyoke Street, opposite State High School No.1, Pyin Oo Lwin
