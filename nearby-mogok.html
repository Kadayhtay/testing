Mogok


      Mogok is a city in the Pyin Oo Lwin District of the Mandalay Region of Myanmar, located 200 km north of Mandalay and 148 km north-east of Shwebo. Mogok was in Shan State and after British, the town was put in Mandalay Region along with Pyin Oo Lwin. Mogok is also referred to as the "Valley of Rubies", a name made famous by the novel Mogok, the Valley of Rubies by Joseph Kessel and Stella Rodway.

Mogok Rubies

     About 90% of rubies in the world market come from Myanmar (Burma) and they are mined in Mogok, Valley of Rubies. There are many other ruby sources in the world such as Sri Lanka and various places in Africa. Only in terms of quality Mogok rubies are best.


  • This amazing necklace, known as the Fai Dee Red Magic, is composed of untreated rubies from both Mogok and Monghsu, Myanmar
  • This Burmese ruby necklace, scheduled for auction at the Hong Kong Sotheby's show on April 7th, 2014 is expected to yield USD $8.7million.
  • The record-breaking gemstone was termed the desired "pigeon's blood" red used for highly sought rubies from Myanmar.
     


     Mogok and other villages nearby have been famous since ancient times for its gemstones, especially ruby and sapphire, but semi-precious stones such as spinel, lapis lazuli, garnet, moonstone, peridot and chrysoberyl are also found.
