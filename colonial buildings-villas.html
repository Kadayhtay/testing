Ancient Villas(Myaing)

Pyin Oo Lwin itself had been founded as a military outpost by the British under the name of May-town in 1896 and later it was obviously used as a cool summer retreat for the wealthy colonial masters at that time. Today Pyin Oo Lwin felt strangely out-of-place somehow with all its European looking red brick houses.

There are many Brithsh style colonial buildings in Pyin Oo Lwin. Most of the colonial houses are situated along the circular road and along the government office road where most government offices are sited at colonial buildings.


Craddock Court (Orchid Nan Myaing Hotel)

This villa is named after the vice governor of Myanmar, Sir Reginald.H.Craddock. It was build in the year 1914 to accomodate British officers during the colonial days. There are 3 spacious accomodation buildings spread out on a vast 15 acres of land. The villa is now open as Orchid Nan Myaing Hotal. Nan Myaing Hotel is loacted in Mandalay-Lashio Road, Pyin Oo Lwin.



Candacraig (Thiri Myaing Hotel)

This villa is originally a guest house of the Bombay Burma Trading Company, which was built in 1904. It is the oldest hotel in Myanmar. It is situated in 6th, Qr, Anawratha Road, Pyin Oo Lwin.



Croxton (Gandamar Myaing)

It is formerly known as 'Croxton' and it is one of the old British mansions converted into hotel. Gandamar Myaing is suited in the 3th Quarter, Nan Dar Road, Pyin Oo Lwin.



Tapsy Villa (Thitsar Myaing)

It is different from other villas in Pyin Oo Lwin. It is the only one villa with tower. It is built in the form of the castles of England. Tapsy Villa is situated at the corner of Tapsy Road (now Thitsar Road) and Circular Road , Pyin Oo LWin.
