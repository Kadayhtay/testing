Governor's House

⚫ Location: Ziwaka Road, behind Aureum Palace Hotel, Pyin Oo Lwin.

The Governor's House was built in 1986 and completed in 1903. It was established for headsquare and also for governors of British to relax in the summer. The Governor's house is located on the hill which is called Governor's hill. There were six successions of governors lived in this governor's house from 1903 to 1942.




Govenors' Photos
  • Sir. Harcourt Butler(1913- 1917)
  • Sir. R.H Dorman Smith (1941-42), Last Governor
  • Sir. Hurbert Rance(1946- 1948)


In 1942, Sir Doman Smith left for Myit Kyi Nar (Kachin State) to escape from Japanese army invasion. The governor's house was left unguarded and destroyed by the Japanese bombing.The building was reduced to rubbles and almost nothing was left. In 2003, Htoo Group of Company had rebuilt it as original building with the help of British Council which provided original old photos. All the constructions was completed in 2005 and has been opened as high class Aureum Palace Resort @ Governor House since 10th December 2006.
